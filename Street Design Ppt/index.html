EXISTING SECTION ANALYSIS

Street Dimensions

ROW: 12 Metres

HF Falaknuma Road is a local collector road serving a dense residential neighbourhood in Juhapura, connecting Makkanagar Road in the north to Sonal Road in the south. The 300m study stretch passes through a high-footfall zone with shops, a community hall, and dense housing.

Despite a 12m ROW — sufficient for a complete street layout — the existing section dedicates nearly all space to motorised vehicles, leaving pedestrians and cyclists without any safe, dedicated space.

CURRENT CONDITION ANALYSIS

Existing Issues

Filter:
01 · CRITICAL

No Footpath

Pedestrians walk on the carriageway. Both sides lack any usable footpath — surfaces are occupied by vendors, construction, and parked two-wheelers.

Impact: Daily safety hazard
02 · CRITICAL

Encroachment & Vendors

Street vendors and building material stack along both carriageway edges, reducing effective road width from 12m to under 8m at peak times.

Impact: Traffic & safety
03 · CRITICAL

No Cycling Infrastructure

Cyclists share mixed traffic with no protection. No lane markings or physical barriers. Connects two residential zones yet offers no safe route.

Impact: High cyclist risk
04 · MODERATE

No Tree Cover

Zero street trees along the 300m stretch. Surface temperatures exceed 42°C in summer creating a severe urban heat island effect on this road.

Impact: Thermal discomfort
05 · MODERATE

Poor Stormwater Drainage

Blocked drains cause waterlogging within 20 minutes of moderate rain. Creates potholes, degrades road surface, and makes walking impossible.

Impact: Infrastructure damage
06 · MODERATE

Zero Accessibility

No tactile paving, kerb ramps, or safe crossings. Persons with disabilities, elderly residents, and children cannot use the street safely.

Impact: Inclusion barrier
07 · LOW

Inadequate Street Lighting

Sparse and poorly positioned street lights leave large sections of the road in darkness after 8 PM, increasing crime risk and pedestrian danger.

Impact: Night-time safety

PROPOSED COMPLETE STREET DESIGN

New Section

12m ROW · Proposed composition

This proposed section reorganises the 12m ROW into a high-quality, pedestrian-oriented street. Left to right, the street provides a 2.0m raised interactive footpath, a 1.0m green MUZ with benches and lighting, a 6.0m two-way carriageway, a 0.5m buffer MUZ, a 1.0m protected cycle track, and a 1.5m residential footpath on the right side.

2m raised footpath on the left side acts as an interaction zone with direct shopfront and vendor access. 1m MUZ follows with greenery, benches and street lights. On the right side there is a 1.5m residential footpath, 1m protected cycle track, 0.5m green buffer MUZ and a 6m carriageway with two-way traffic and no divider.

THE COMPLETE STREET VISION

HF Falaknuma Road · Transformed

A 12m ROW redesigned to serve everyone — pedestrians, cyclists, and vehicles equally.

Wide Footpaths

1.5m continuous, level, tactile paved footpath on both sides with unobstructed pedestrian flow

Protected Cycle Tracks

1.0m physically segregated cycle lanes on each side, painted green with bollard protection

Organised Traffic

7m carriageway with lane markings, speed calming measures and organised 2-way traffic flow

Street Tree Canopy

Native shade trees planted at 8m spacing on both sides — reducing heat island by up to 4°C

LED Street Lighting

Solar-powered LED luminaires at 15m spacing ensuring safe visibility for all users after dark

Universal Accessibility

Kerb ramps at every junction, tactile paths for the visually impaired and accessible pedestrian crossings

Street Furniture

Benches, waste bins, water fountains and cycle stands integrated within the footpath zone
